Who was Oskar Schindler?

Oskar was a german industrialist who saved approximately 1100 human lifes from the Nazis, he employeed all these people in his factories.

A businessman with taste by the good things of life, Schindler didn't look to be someone whose was going to become the savior of so many lives.

After the german invasion in Polony, he moved to Cracovia. Taking advantage of the german program to arianize the Polonian companies, in november of 1939 he bought a factory of enamelled nails and reopened it with the name of Emalia. Although Schindler had two other factories, just in Emalia he employeed jews slaves, brought from a ghetto next to the city. During the close of the ghetto, in march of 1943, Schindler allowed your jew workers to stay in relative security in the factory during the night.
